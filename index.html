My research interests include quantitative genetics, population genomics, genomics, and molecular ecology. I've been working for the past few years with the lovable Caenorhabditis elegans, a small free-living nematode species that has been central to no less than six Noble prizes! My research is focused on understanding how evolution has shaped the patterns of natural genetic diversity in the species and learning about the roles that specific genetic variants play in influencing fitness related traits.

If you're interested in learning more about my work, check out my projects or publications pages. Just want to chat? Don't hesitate to reach out!
